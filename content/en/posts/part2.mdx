Embark on the second part of your 'Your Name' pilgrimage, focusing on the iconic Minato Ward in Tokyo. This district, known for its modern architecture and vibrant atmosphere, hosts several key locations that brought the beloved anime to life.

Our first stop is **The National Art Center, Tokyo** in Roppongi. This distinctive building served as the setting for Taki's part-time job at the Italian restaurant. To get here, take the Tokyo Metro Chiyoda Line to Nogizaka Station (Exit 6) or the Hibiya Line/Oedo Line to Roppongi Station (Exit 7). The exterior, with its unique glass facade, is instantly recognizable. For the best photo angles, try capturing the building from across the street, focusing on its wave-like structure. Inside, while the specific restaurant might not be identical, the general ambiance and scale of the atrium evoke the film's aesthetic. Be mindful of museum visitors and avoid using flash photography if you enter the exhibition areas. Weekday mornings are ideal for fewer crowds.

Next, head to **Roppongi Hills**, a short walk from The National Art Center. The **Tokyo City View observation deck** and its open-air Sky Deck offer breathtaking panoramic views of Tokyo, including the iconic Tokyo Tower and the NTT Docomo Yoyogi Building, both prominently featured in the anime's establishing shots. Access Roppongi Hills via Roppongi Station (Hibiya Line, Exit 1C; Oedo Line, Exit 3). The Sky Deck provides an unparalleled opportunity to recreate the vast cityscapes seen in 'Your Name'. Aim for late afternoon to capture both daylight views and the stunning sunset, followed by the city lights twinkling at night. Tripods are generally allowed on the Sky Deck, but be considerate of other visitors. Check the weather forecast, as the Sky Deck may close during strong winds or rain.

Finally, no Minato Ward 'Your Name' tour is complete without acknowledging **Tokyo Tower**. While not a direct scene location for character interaction, its presence is a constant backdrop in the film, symbolizing Tokyo itself. You can view it spectacularly from Roppongi Hills, or get up close by taking the Oedo Line to Akabanebashi Station (Exit Akabanebashi) or the Hibiya Line to Kamiyacho Station (Exit 1). For ground-level shots, **Shiba Park**, located directly beneath the tower, offers excellent perspectives, especially with the tower framed by trees. Early morning provides soft light and fewer tourists. Remember to respect local residents and businesses, especially when taking photos in public areas. Avoid obstructing pathways or private entrances. Always prioritize your safety and be aware of your surroundings, particularly in busy urban environments. This concludes your Minato Ward 'Your Name' journey, offering a blend of cinematic nostalgia and Tokyo's modern charm.
