Uji, a city steeped in history and renowned for its exquisite green tea, holds a special place in the hearts of "Sound! Euphonium" fans. This guide focuses on a crucial segment of the anime pilgrimage, covering iconic spots like Kumiko's Bench, Agata Shrine, and JR Uji Station, all central to the series' narrative and visual identity. Prepare to immerse yourself in the world of Kitauji High's brass band.

Your journey typically begins at **JR Uji Station**. To get here, take the JR Nara Line from Kyoto Station (approximately 17 minutes by rapid train) or from Osaka (transfer at Kyoto). The station itself is a frequent backdrop in the anime, often seen as characters arrive or depart Uji. For photography, capture the station's modern exterior, the platforms, and the ticket gates, trying to match angles from the anime. Early mornings or late evenings are best to avoid commuter crowds, allowing for clearer shots. Always be mindful of other passengers and avoid obstructing pathways.

From JR Uji Station, a pleasant 10-15 minute walk will lead you to the iconic **Kumiko's Bench**. Head towards the Uji River and cross the Uji Bridge. The bench is located along the riverbank, offering picturesque views of the water and the bridge. This spot is famous for numerous heartfelt conversations between Kumiko and Reina, making it a must-visit for fans. When shooting, try to replicate the characters' sitting positions and expressions. Frame your shots to include the Uji River and the distinctive Uji Bridge in the background. Late afternoon provides beautiful soft light, perfect for photography. This bench is a public space, so be respectful of others who wish to use it. Avoid monopolizing it for extended periods, especially during peak tourist times.

Continuing your pilgrimage, a short walk from Kumiko's Bench will bring you to **Agata Shrine (県神社)**. This shrine plays a significant role in several anime episodes, particularly during festival scenes. The shrine's tranquil grounds and traditional architecture offer a serene contrast to the bustling riverfront. Focus your photography on the main torii gate, the shrine's main hall, and the surrounding forested areas. Look for specific angles that appeared during the Agata Festival scenes. The shrine is generally open during daylight hours. If you visit during the actual Agata Festival in June, expect large crowds and a vibrant atmosphere, which can be both a blessing and a challenge for photography. Remember that this is a sacred site; maintain a respectful demeanor, keep your voice down, and refrain from using flash photography inside any shrine buildings. Remove hats when entering the main worship areas.

**General Etiquette and Safety:** Uji is a very walkable city, so comfortable shoes are essential. While exploring, remember to support local businesses, perhaps by trying some Uji matcha. Always be aware of your surroundings, especially when crossing streets. The best times to visit Uji for a pilgrimage are spring (for cherry blossoms) or autumn (for fall foliage), which add extra beauty to the anime's settings. However, any time of year offers a chance to connect with the world of "Sound! Euphonium." Re-watching relevant episodes before your trip can help you identify specific scenes and angles, enhancing your pilgrimage experience.
