Embark on the third part of your "Your Name" (Kimi no Na wa) anime pilgrimage, venturing from the charming town of Hida Furukawa in Gifu Prefecture to the breathtaking Lake Suwa in Nagano Prefecture. These locations served as profound inspirations for the film's iconic settings, offering fans a chance to step directly into Taki and Mitsuha's world. This guide provides practical details to ensure a smooth and memorable journey.

**Hida Furukawa, Gifu: Stepping into Itomori's Surroundings**

**Transit:** Hida Furukawa is easily accessible by train. From Takayama, it's just a short 15-minute ride on the JR Takayama Main Line. If coming from further afield, you'll likely connect via Nagoya or Toyama to Takayama first. The town itself is compact, and most pilgrimage spots are within walking distance from Hida Furukawa Station.

**Key Pilgrimage Spots & Photography Tips:**

1.  **Hida Furukawa Station (飛騨古川駅):** This station is instantly recognizable from the scene where Taki and his friends arrive in search of Itomori.
    *   **Shooting Angle:** Position yourself on the platform to capture the train pulling in or departing, mimicking the anime's perspective. The station sign is also a must-photograph. Be mindful of train schedules and platform safety.
    *   **Timing:** Early morning or late afternoon can offer softer light and fewer crowds. Check train arrival/departure times to capture the dynamic scenes.

2.  **Hida City Library (飛騨市図書館):** The interior of this library was the direct inspiration for the library where Taki researches the comet and Itomori.
    *   **Etiquette:** This is a working public library. **Crucially, photography inside is strictly prohibited without prior permission.** Respect the rules and the privacy of library users. You can admire the exterior and perhaps the entrance area, but do not attempt to take photos inside. This is a prime example of respecting local rules during a pilgrimage.

3.  **Keta Wakamiya Shrine (気多若宮神社):** While the Miyamizu Shrine itself is fictional, Keta Wakamiya Shrine in Hida Furukawa is widely believed to be a significant inspiration for its architectural style and atmosphere.
    *   **Shooting Angle:** Focus on the traditional wooden structures, the stone steps, and the surrounding forest. Try to capture the serene, ancient feel.
    *   **Timing:** Any time of day is suitable, but the morning light can beautifully illuminate the shrine.

4.  **Ajidokoro Kyo-ya (味処古川):** This restaurant is famous for its "Gochisou-sama" scene where Taki and his friends enjoy local Hida beef.
    *   **Experience:** Enjoy a meal here, savoring the local cuisine just like the characters. It's a great way to immerse yourself in the local culture.
    *   **Etiquette:** Be a respectful customer. Photography of your food is generally fine, but be discreet if photographing the interior, especially if it's busy.

**General Hida Furukawa Tips:**
*   **Safety:** The town is generally safe, but always be aware of your surroundings, especially near roads and train tracks.
*   **Local Charm:** Take time to wander the streets, admire the white-walled storehouses, and interact with locals.

**Lake Suwa, Nagano: The Heart of Itomori**

**Transit:** Reaching Lake Suwa, specifically Kamisuwa, from Hida Furukawa involves a longer journey. You'll typically take the JR Takayama Main Line back to Takayama, then a bus or train to Matsumoto, and finally the JR Chuo Main Line to Kamisuwa Station. The entire journey can take 3-4 hours, so plan accordingly. Alternatively, if coming from Tokyo, it's a direct train ride on the JR Chuo Main Line (Limited Express Azusa) to Kamisuwa Station, taking about 2.5 hours.

**Key Pilgrimage Spots & Photography Tips:**

1.  **Lake Suwa (諏訪湖):** The iconic, comet-scarred lake of Itomori is directly inspired by Lake Suwa.
    *   **Shooting Angle:** The lake can be viewed from various points along its shore. Kamisuwa Station offers glimpses, but the best panoramic views are from higher ground.
    *   **Timing:** While beautiful at any time, the lake truly shines at sunset.

2.  **Kamisuwa Station (上諏訪駅):** The station itself offers views of the lake and is a convenient starting point for exploring the area.
    *   **Unique Feature:** Don't miss the footbath (ashiyu) on Platform 1, a perfect way to relax after your journey.

3.  **Tateishi Park (立石公園):** This is arguably the most crucial "Your Name" pilgrimage spot in Suwa. The panoramic view of Lake Suwa from here is almost identical to the aerial shots of Itomori Lake in the anime.
    *   **Shooting Angle:** Position yourself at the main observation deck. Experiment with wide-angle shots to capture the entire lake and the surrounding town.
    *   **Timing:** **Absolutely essential to visit at sunset.** The magical twilight colors over the lake, mirroring the anime's most poignant scenes, are unforgettable. Arrive at least 30-45 minutes before sunset to secure a good spot, as it can get crowded.
    *   **Transit to Tateishi Park:** From Kamisuwa Station, it's about a 15-20 minute uphill walk, or a short taxi ride. The walk can be steep, so wear comfortable shoes.

**General Suwa Tips:**
*   **Accommodation:** Consider staying overnight in Kamisuwa to fully experience the sunset at Tateishi Park and explore the hot spring town.
*   **Etiquette:** At Tateishi Park, be mindful of other visitors, especially during sunset. Avoid blocking views and be patient.
*   **Safety:** The walk to Tateishi Park involves roads, so be aware of traffic.

**Conclusion:**
This two-part journey from Hida Furukawa to Lake Suwa offers an unparalleled immersion into the world of "Your Name." By respecting local customs, planning your transit, and timing your visits, you can capture stunning photographs and create lasting memories of these truly magical anime pilgrimage sites.
